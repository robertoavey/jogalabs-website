Chapters: Intro | Worst Team in History → New Manager Losing More Games Than The Last → I'm not going to change → We need to survive this moment → Worst Team Ever - Not How to Be a Leader → You Want Headlines → Press Training is needed for Amorim → Everybody is underperforming → I know it was going to be difficult → If You Are A Leader of a NEW Team Please LISTEN TO ME

Content: Roberto reacts to Ruben Amorim's press conference clips ("we are being the worst team maybe in history of Manchester United"). Points made:
- "Whoa, don't hold back — is that how you feel about your team?" A new coach losing more than the last coach is 60% of the problem; players don't want you; you can't play Amorim-style football 100% — do it 50% until your new players arrive; refusing to change while seeing the iceberg ahead = Titanic.
- Amorim: "I know we can succeed but we need to survive this moment... we are being the worst team maybe in history of Manchester United."
- Roberto: imagine in corporate or your own business, a new leader walks in and says "my team sucks" — you'll get nothing out of your players. Say whatever behind closed doors, in the locker room, on the training pitch — never in front of everyone.
- "You want headlines... here are your headlines: MY TEAM SUCKS. Has this guy had any press training? This is insane." Football is a business like corporate — leadership positions require leadership training, no matter how long you've been kicking a ball.
- If you're cheeky off the cuff, you'd better demonstrate it on the pitch.
- Amorim: "Everybody here is underperforming... it's unacceptable to lose so many games for any club in the Premier League, imagine Manchester United... we have to continue, there is no other way, we need to suffer... I knew it was going to be hard to put a completely new idea in the moment, but when you lose games you don't have that luck... we are going to suffer because I will continue to do the same."
- Roberto's leadership lessons if you lead a NEW team: don't badmouth your team — ever; get the most out of your people; change responsibilities and roles, move people around; don't be hard-headed or stubborn; roll with the punches, pivot, change.
- Closer: "Amorim, we're thankful you're here at Manchester United, you're definitely better than the last guy... well, I think anybody could have been better than the last guy."
